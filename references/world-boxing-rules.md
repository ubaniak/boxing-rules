# World Boxing Competition Rules
**In Force: February 2024**
Source: https://worldboxing.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/World-Boxing-Competition-Rules-Feb-2024-v3.99-ClEAN.pdf

---

## Rule 1: The Competitions

### 1.1 Competition and Bout Format
1.1.1 World Boxing Competitions generally use a single-elimination format.
1.1.2 A Competition usually covers multiple days.
1.1.3 Men and Women Boxers compete in their respective Age and Weight Categories.
1.1.4 Boxers aim to progress through preliminary rounds, quarter-finals, semi-finals and the finals.
1.1.5 Within a Competition, each Bout consists of three (3) rounds and two (2) rest periods between the rounds.
1.1.6 The duration of the rounds is:
- Three (3) minutes for Elite and U19 Age Category Competitions
- Two (2) minutes for U17 Age Category Competitions
1.1.7 The duration of the rest periods between rounds is one (1) minute for all Competitions.

### 1.2 World Boxing Competitions
1.2.1 World Boxing Competitions are boxing competitions either owned or sanctioned by World Boxing.
1.2.2 Competitions owned by World Boxing: World Boxing Championships (Elite), U19 World Boxing Championships, Continental Boxing Championships (Elite), World Boxing Cup Finals, World Boxing Cup.
1.2.3 Competitions sanctioned by World Boxing: Boxing competitions at Multi-Sport Games; U17, U19 and other non-Elite Continental Boxing Championships; World Boxing Challenge.
1.2.4 All World Boxing Competitions will be listed on the official website of World Boxing.

### 1.3 International Competitions
1.3.1 International Competitions are Boxing Competitions organised by National Federations affiliated to World Boxing or by World Boxing recognised partners.
1.3.2 An International Competition is owned by the Host National Federation.
1.3.3 All International Competitions recognised by World Boxing will be listed on the official website.
1.3.4 Entry to International Competitions should be open to all National Federations affiliated to World Boxing.
1.3.5 Boxers will be entered by their National Federations into International Competitions.
1.3.6 LOCs will only accept entries that comply with the World Boxing Statutes and Competition Rules.
1.3.7 A minimum of thirty (30) boxers from four (4) National Federations must participate in an International Competition to have its results confirmed.

### 1.4 National Competitions
1.4.1 National Competitions are boxing competitions organised by National Federations within their territory without any involvement of World Boxing.
1.4.2 National Competitions can only feature boxers affiliated to the Host National Federation.
1.4.3 National Competitions are organised in accordance with the rules of the Host National Federation.

### 1.5 World Boxing Championships
1.5.1 Entries should be open to all National Federations affiliated to World Boxing.
1.5.2 World Boxing Championships will be held in the year following the Summer Olympic Games and every odd year between Summer Olympic Games.
1.5.3 U19 World Boxing Championships will be held in the year of the Summer Olympic Games and every even year between Summer Olympic Games.
1.5.4 At least fifteen (15) National Federations from three Continents must have been entered in the Final Entries for validity.
1.5.5 For a Weight Category to be valid, at least six (6) Boxers from two (2) continents must compete.
1.5.6 Hosts will be selected by World Boxing.
1.5.7 Maximum number of participating Boxers may be restricted per Event Rules.

### 1.6 Continental Boxing Championships
1.6.1 Entries should be open to all National Federations affiliated to World Boxing and the respective Continental Confederation.
1.6.2 Continental Boxing Championships will be held every even year.
1.6.3 In Africa, America, Asia and Europe, at least ten (10) National Federations must be in Final Entries.
1.6.4 In Oceania, at least four (4) National Federations must be in Final Entries.
1.6.5 For a Weight Category to be valid, at least four (4) Boxers must compete.
1.6.6 Hosts will be selected by World Boxing.
1.6.7 Maximum number of participating Boxers may be restricted per Event Rules.

### 1.10 World Boxing Ranking
1.10.1 World Boxing Ranking may be used to determine seeding at World Boxing's major Elite competitions.
1.10.2 World Boxing Ranking will be applicable only to the Elite Age Category Boxers.
1.10.3 World Boxing Rankings will be categorised by gender and Weight Categories.
1.10.4 Ranking points cannot be transferred to a different weight category.
1.10.5 To be included in the Ranking, Boxers must have no current suspensions or sanctions.
1.10.6 Ranking points can be earned at: Olympic Games, World Boxing Championships, Continental Games, Continental Boxing Championships, World Boxing Cup Finals, World Boxing Cup, World Boxing Challenge.
1.10.7 The World Boxing Rankings began with the 2024 Paris Olympic Games and operates on a three-year, rolling point system.
1.10.8 Points are maintained for three calendar years, decreasing by a set percentage on 1 January of each year.
1.10.9 Total points reduced by 25% on 1 January of the following year, 50% on 1 January of the second year, 100% on 1 January of the third year.
1.10.10 Points Distribution:
- Olympic Games: 1st=2000, 2nd=1300, 3rd=800, 4th=800, 5th-8th=500
- World Boxing Championships: 1st=1500, 2nd=1000, 3rd=600, 4th=600, 5th-8th=300
- Continental Multi-Sport Games / Continental Boxing Championships: 1st=800, 2nd=500, 3rd=300, 4th=300, 5th-8th=150
- World Boxing Cup Finals: 1st=300, 2nd=200, 3rd=150, 4th=150
- World Boxing Cup: 1st=150, 2nd=100, 3rd=75, 4th=75
- World Boxing Challenge: 1st=50, 2nd=30, 3rd=15, 4th=15
1.10.11 A Boxer may not accumulate ranking points from more than four (4) competitions at a given time.
1.10.12 Total points based on the four (4) competitions with the highest points within the three-year rolling system.
1.10.13 Boxers sanctioned for Anti-Doping violation will not receive ranking points for affected events.
1.10.14 Ranking points are non-transferable.
1.10.15 Tiebreaker: the boxer who earned more points at the highest tier event is ranked higher.

### 1.11 Seeding
1.11.1 Seeding may be applied for: Olympic Games, World Boxing Championships, Continental Games, Continental Boxing Championships, World Boxing Cup Finals, World Boxing Cup.
1.11.2 Seeding will be based on the World Boxing Ranking System.
1.11.3 Decision on whether seeding applies will be taken by World Boxing Executive Board.

### 1.12 Medal Ceremonies
1.12.1 Boxers need to compete in at least one bout to be awarded a medal.
1.12.2 Medal Ceremonies will be conducted in accordance with World Boxing Event Rules.
1.12.3 Medal Ceremonies should be conducted as soon as possible after the competition is complete.
1.12.4 Bronze medals will be presented first, followed by Silver, then Gold.
1.12.5 No headgear or national flags are allowed during the Medal Ceremonies.
1.12.6 No statements, signs, demonstrations, objects, or additional people are permitted during Medal Ceremonies.

### 1.13 Prize Money
1.13.1 Competitions may offer prize money either to a Boxer or to a National Federation.
1.13.2 Prize money amounts must be agreed with World Boxing Executive Board prior to the competition.
1.13.3 Prize money will be distributed by electronic bank transfer, no later than 30 days after the competition.
1.13.4 Prize money may not be distributed to a Boxer awaiting the result of an Anti-Doping Test.
1.13.5 Prize money may not be distributed while there is any legal proceeding open against the Boxer or National Federation.
1.13.6 Prize money may be offset against any unsettled balance toward World Boxing, Continental Confederation, or the LOC.

## Rule 2: The Boxers

### 2.1 Age Categories
2.1.1 The age of a Boxer is determined using their calendar year of birth.
2.1.2 Men and Women Boxers between the ages of 19 to 40 are categorised as Elite Boxers.
2.1.3 Men and Women Boxers between the ages of 17 to 18 are categorised as U19 Boxers.
2.1.4 Boys and Girls Boxers between the ages of 15 to 16 are categorised as U17 Boxers.
2.1.5 Boys and Girls Boxers between the ages of 13 to 14 are categorised as U15 Boxers. Regulated by Confederations and/or National Federations.
2.1.6 Men and Women Boxers over the age of 40 are categorised as Masters Boxers. Regulated by Confederations and/or National Federations.

### 2.3 Sport Nationality
2.3.1 Boxers must be of the same nationality as the National Federation they represent with a valid passport and/or citizen certificate.
2.3.2 Boxers must be affiliated to the National Federation that has entered them.
2.3.3 Every Boxer must be of the same nationality as the National Federation entering them, with the exception of recognized refugees.
2.3.4 Nationality is confirmed by passport and/or citizen certificate.
2.3.5 Elite and U19 Boxers who represented one country and changed nationality may represent their new country provided at least three (3) years have passed since their last bout for their former country in a World Boxing Competition. This period may be reduced to one (1) calendar year with agreement of both National Federations and World Boxing Executive Board.
2.3.6 Where a new National Federation is formed and the Boxer has a passport for that territory, they may elect to represent the new country without delay (this choice may only be made once).
2.3.7 In Multi-Sport Games, Sport Nationality may be subject to the rules of the governing bodies.
2.3.8 The National Federation is responsible for ensuring Boxers meet Sport Nationality requirements.
2.3.9 World Boxing Executive Board will make final decisions regarding Sport Nationality.
2.3.10 Any change of Sport Nationality must be submitted to World Boxing by 31 October of the year before the Boxer wants to compete for the new country.

### 2.4 Refugee Status
2.4.1 Boxers recognised as refugees by UNHCR may be entered in Boxing Competitions and will compete under the World Boxing flag as RBT (Refugee Boxing Team).
2.4.2 All requests from Refugee Boxers must be sent to the World Boxing Executive Board.
2.4.3 Refugee Boxers hold the same status as Boxers representing National Federations.
2.4.4 If the Refugee Boxer obtains a Sport Nationality, the World Boxing Sport Nationality rules apply.
2.4.5 Refugee Boxers may compete in National Competitions in the country where they obtained UN Refugee status.

### 2.5 Medical Eligibility
2.5.1 All allowed disabilities and prohibited conditions are outlined in the World Boxing Medical Rules.
2.5.2 All medical probation periods must be respected at all Competitions.
2.5.3 To return to competing after a probation period, the Boxer must present an updated Medical Certificate and be classified as "fit to box" by a doctor.

## Rule 3: The Seconds

### 3.1 Duties and Permissions
3.1.1 The National Federation is responsible for ensuring that their delegated Seconds possess the appropriate skills, knowledge and certification.
3.1.2 Up to three (3) Seconds can accompany a Boxer in the corner during a Bout. Only two (2) Seconds may mount the apron of the ring and only one (1) may enter the ring during rest periods between rounds. Seconds are not allowed to enter the ring outside of rest periods.
3.1.3 Seconds must leave the ring and the apron prior to the start of each round, and must remove seats, towels, buckets, etc.
3.1.4 Seconds must have a towel for the Boxer. A Second may indicate the retirement of the Boxer by mounting the apron and throwing the towel into the ring (except when the Referee is counting).
3.1.5 Seconds must use the transparent bottled water whenever it is provided by the LOC.
3.1.6 In the case of a cut, Seconds are only allowed to administer adrenaline 1/1000 and non-petroleum skin-protective jelly during the Bout.
3.1.7 Seconds may not administer any drugs aside from the approved ones during the Bout.
3.1.8 Seconds may carry Ice bags, Enswell, 4x4 gauze padding and swabs.
3.1.9 Seconds may use one (1) bag with maximum dimensions of 30cm x 20cm x 20cm when working in the field-of-play.

### 3.2 Prohibited Actions
3.2.1 Seconds must remain seated during the rounds in the designated seating area.
3.2.2 Seconds are not permitted to encourage or incite spectators by words or signs while in the Field of Play.
3.2.3 Seconds are not allowed to leave their designated area, touch the ring, clap, address Technical Officials, opposing Boxer or Seconds during a bout.
3.2.4 Seconds are not allowed to cause any type of scandal in the Field of Play or take any unsportsmanlike action.
3.2.5 Seconds are not allowed to use cameras or communication devices in the Field of Play (smartphones, tablets, walkie-talkies, headsets, radios, etc.).
3.2.6 Seconds are not allowed to administer supplemental oxygen or any type of inhaler to a boxer during a bout.

### 3.3 Attire
3.3.1 At World Boxing Competitions, Seconds should be identified by wearing their respective national uniform.
3.3.2 Seconds are not limited to full tracksuits; they may wear shorts (at or below the knees), vests, T-shirts and caps.
3.3.3 Seconds' Uniform must not have any political, religious or personal slogans, statements or images of illegal substances, or sponsorship or advertising other than the manufacturer's logo.

## Rule 4: The Team Leaders

### 4.1 Role and Duties
4.1.1 Each Team will have a Team Leader designated by the respective National Federation.
4.1.2 Team Coaches, Physios and other Team Officials can perform the duties of the Team Leader when designated.
4.1.3 Team Leaders will attend Boxing Entries Check, Technical Meeting and the Official Boxers' Draw.
4.1.4 Team Leaders are responsible for the behaviour of their Teams during the Event.
4.1.5 Team Leaders will notify the LOC and World Boxing of any changes of the boxer's participation in bouts.
4.1.6 A Team Leader may appoint a replacement, which must be confirmed by the LOC and World Boxing.

## Rule 5: Pre-Competition Activities

### 5.1 Entries
5.1.1 National Federations are responsible for all entries to World Boxing Competitions.
5.1.2 National Federations are responsible for the validity of all information and documents.
5.1.3 National Federations will ensure that their Boxers compete under the correct Sport Nationality.
5.1.4 National Federations are responsible to ensure health, fitness and skill levels of Boxers.
5.1.5 Only one Boxer per Weight Category per country is allowed to compete, unless otherwise specified.
5.1.6 All Team entries must comply with requirements including: copy of passport or refugee ID, signed Code of Conduct, Medical Certificate (within previous 3 months), Signed Declaration of Non-Pregnancy for Women Boxers (within previous 1 month).
5.1.7 Boxers whose National Federation has been suspended or expelled may not be accepted as entries.
5.1.8 Boxers serving Ineligibility or provisional suspension for anti-doping violation cannot be entered.
5.1.9 The Boxer may not compete under any other name than that confirmed by World Boxing.
5.1.14 Entries for World Boxing Competitions will be made in two phases:
- Preliminary entries: at least six (6) weeks before the competition
- Final entries: no later than seven (7) days preceding the Event
5.1.17 After the Final entries deadline, substitutions may only be made with express permission of the Technical Delegate.
5.1.19 National Federations whose Team fails to attend or compete will be fined 50 Euros per Boxer that was entered but did not compete.
5.1.20 National Federations whose Team fails to attend or compete will be liable to reimburse the LOC for financial loss incurred.

### 5.2 Boxing Entries Check
5.2.1 All teams must attend the Boxing Entries Check Meeting to validate team entries before the Official Boxers' Draw. Failure to attend may lead to complete withdrawal of the Team.
5.2.5 To confirm the entries, teams must present: Passport or refugee ID, Boxer's Record Book, Signed Conditions of Participation Form, Medical Certificate (within previous 3 months), Signed Declaration of Non-Pregnancy for Women Boxers (within previous 1 month), one set of Boxers' uniforms.

### 5.5 Competition Schedule
5.5.1 Following the Official Boxers' Draw the Technical Delegate will approve and the LOC will publish the Competition Schedule.
5.5.3 Any amendments must be communicated to the teams at least one (1) day before the affected Bouts.
5.5.5 A break of twelve (12) hours is mandatory between Bouts. A Boxer must not compete in more than one (1) Bout within the same calendar day.

### 5.6 Weigh-ins
5.6.1 Weigh-ins generally take place in the mornings during competition days and Boxers are required to make weight on the day they box.
5.6.3 A Boxer is allowed to be present at the official scales only once.
5.6.4 With the body naked or using only underwear, the weight displayed on the scale is the Boxer's official weight.
5.6.5 A Boxer's weight must not exceed the maximum limit of the Boxer's Weight Category. Exceeding results in a walkover in favour of the opponent.
5.6.6 At the first official weigh-in of a Competition, the Boxer's weight must exceed the minimum limit. Weighing in below the minimum results in a walkover in favour of the opponent.
5.6.7 Electronic scales must be used for both Test scales and Daily Weigh-In scales, calibrated and not moved after calibration.
5.6.9 Separate areas or separate time slots must be allocated for Men and Women Boxers. Technical Officials checking the weight of Boxers must be of the same gender as the Boxers.
5.6.11 Besides Boxers, team representatives are not allowed in Weigh-in areas or rooms.
5.6.12 The time from the end of the Daily Weigh-In to the start of the first Bout of the day must not be less than three (3) hours.

## Rule 6: The Referee and Judges (R&Js)

### 6.1 Role
6.1.1 Each Bout must be controlled by a Referee who will not score the Bout. Five (5) Judges will be assigned and will score each round ringside.
6.1.2 Referee and Judges must be certified by World Boxing to be appointed to any World Boxing Competition.

### 6.2 Duties and Permissions of the Judge
6.2.1 Judges will score independently with the "10-Point Must" system.
6.2.2 Judges will not speak or give any sign to Boxers, Seconds, other Judges, or to anyone else before, during or after the Bout, until they have left the Field of Play.
6.2.3 Judges will not leave their seat until the final Bout decision has been announced.

### 6.3 Duties and Permissions of the Referee
6.3.1 Referees are primarily concerned for the safety of both Boxers throughout the Bout.
6.3.2 Referees will ensure that Competition Rules and fair play are strictly observed.
6.3.3 Referees will maintain control of the Bout at all stages.
6.3.4 Referees will seek to prevent a weak Boxer from receiving undue and unnecessary punishment.
6.3.5 Referees will not exit the ring until the final Bout decision has been announced and both Boxers have exited.

### 6.4 Dress Code
6.4.1 Referees and Judges must wear: White, short-sleeved, button-down collared shirt; black bow tie; black trousers (no denim); black sport shoes (no heels); surgical gloves.

### 6.5 Bout Assignment
6.5.1 Referees and Judges will be selected using the approved Electronic Draw System.
6.5.2 Each Referee and Judge will be of a different nationality from each other and from both Boxers.
6.5.3 A Referee or Judge will not be a national or resident of any country that is a dominion, colony or dependency of any Boxer's country.
6.5.4 A Referee or Judge who changed their nationality will not officiate in a bout where a Boxer of their former nationality is taking part.

## Rule 7: Judging a Bout

### 7.1 Scoring System
7.1.1 An Approved World Boxing Scoring System will be used in all Bouts.
7.1.2 The approved Scoring System is based on the "Ten Point Must" System.

### 7.2 Scoring Criteria
7.2.1 There are three (3) Scoring Criteria:
- Number of quality blows on target area
- Domination of the Bout by technical and tactical superiority
- Competitiveness

7.2.2 For a blow to be counted as a quality blow on target area:
- The blow must connect in the correct area.
- The blow must have the weight of the body or shoulder behind it.
- The blow must connect with the knuckle surface of the glove.
- The blow must connect without infringing a rule.
- The blow must be cleanly connected.
- The Judge must have clear vision of the punch.

7.2.2.1 Quantity and quality of blows should be considered the most important factor.
7.2.2.2 When the quantity of blows is very close to equal, the quality of blows may decide the winner of the round.

7.2.3 Domination by technical and tactical superiority includes:
- A Boxer controlling the Bout with a combination of attack and defence.
- A Boxer who is the "Effective Aggressor" (constant forward movement alone does not qualify).
- A Boxer who forces the action and sets the tempo.
- A Boxer who causes their opponent to miss and makes the opponent vulnerable to counter punches.
- A Boxer who sets the positions in the ring (lateral movement, etc.).
- A Boxer who neutralises the style of their opponent (slugger vs boxer, southpaw vs orthodox, etc.).
- A Boxer who successfully avoids clean hits by blocking, slipping, weaving, parrying and footwork.

7.2.4 Competitiveness is determined by continuously showing initiative, a strong desire to compete and to win.

### 7.3 Scoring Allocation
7.3.1 10-9 – Close round: when both Boxers are evenly matched in most criteria. A close round may occur even with a standing Eight Count or a Knockdown.
7.3.2 10-8 – Clear winner: there is a clear winner due to large difference in quality blows, or a smaller difference with also a standing Eight Count or a Knockdown.
7.3.3 10-7 – Total dominance: one Boxer is completely dominant in all criteria, with a large difference in quality blows and possibly a standing Eight Count or a Knockdown.

### 7.4 End of Each Round
7.4.1 Each Judge independently judges and awards ten (10) points to the winner and nine (9) or fewer to the loser.
7.4.2 Seven (7) is the minimum score to the losing Boxer.
7.4.3 Every round must have a declared winner.
7.4.4 There can be no 10-10 rounds.
7.4.5 The Judges must push the button on the scoring pad within five (5) seconds after the bell rings.
7.4.6 Scores are visible on public display and in the Seconds' corners, but NOT visible to Referees and Judges in the Field of Play.

### 7.5 End of the Bout
7.5.1 Scores are announced immediately at the end of the Bout after approval by the Deputy Technical Delegate.
7.5.2 After the winner is announced, the precise scores and total scores are identified on a public display.

## Rule 8: Refereeing a Bout

### 8.1 Commands and Signs
8.1.1 The Referee controls the bout with four (4) words of command:
- "Stop" – ordering the Boxers to stop boxing
- "Box" – ordering the Boxers to start or continue boxing
- "Break" – used when breaking a clinch
- "Time" – ordering the Timekeeper and Gong Operator to stop the time, and Boxers to stop boxing

8.1.2 Following "Stop" or "Time", the Referee indicates any infringement using explanatory signs, verbal commands and/or gestures.
8.1.3 The Referee may touch a Boxer with the hand to stop and to break the Bout, or to separate the Boxers.

### 8.2 Prior to the Start of the Bout
8.2.1 Before inviting the boxers to enter the ring, the Referee ensures that the ring is visually safe.
8.2.2 The Referee ensures all ringside Judges and Ringside Doctors positions are occupied.
8.2.3 Once the Boxers have entered the ring, the Referee checks if the Boxers are wearing appropriate uniforms and boxing equipment.
8.2.4 The Referee brings both Boxers to the centre of the ring for pre-bout instructions.
8.2.5 The Referee signals the Deputy Technical Delegate to ensure the Bout can start and awaits confirmation, then signals the Timekeeper and Gong Operator to ring the gong and commands "Box".

### 8.3 During the Bout
8.3.1 The Referee cautions a Boxer or administers a warning regarding fouls or for any other reason in the interest of fair play.
8.3.2 In case of a knockdown, the Referee ensures that the opponent immediately goes to the neutral corner and remains there until the Referee allows the Boxer to leave.
8.3.3 The Referee terminates a contest at any stage if it is one-sided and risking a Boxer's safety.
8.3.4 The Referee terminates a contest at any stage if one of the Boxers has received an injury that the Referee decides the Boxer should not continue.
8.3.5 The Referee terminates a contest at any stage if the Boxers are inactive, not competitive, not in earnest. In such a case, the Referee may disqualify one or both Boxers.
8.3.6 The Referee may disqualify a Boxer who fails to comply immediately with the Referee's command, or behaves in an offensive or aggressive manner at any time.
8.3.7 The Referee disqualifies a Boxer for committing a serious foul with or without previous warning.
8.3.9 The Referee stops the Bout in the event of a Boxer's equipment becoming undone during a Bout.

### 8.4 After the End of the Bout
8.4.1 The Referee ensures that the Boxer has appropriate bandages after the end of the Bout.
8.4.2 The Referee indicates the winner of the Bout by raising a Boxer's hand only after the announcement has been made.
8.4.3 When the winner is announced, the Referee must be positioned in the middle of the ring holding one hand of each Boxer and raise the hand of the winning Boxer while facing the main TV camera.
8.4.4 In case the Referee decides to stop the Bout, they must first inform the Deputy Technical Delegate of the decision and reason.
8.4.5 The Referee may consult the Ringside Doctor regarding a competing Boxer's condition.
8.4.6 When the Referee calls the Ringside Doctor to examine a Boxer, only the Referee and the Ringside Doctor will be present in the ring or on the apron.
8.4.7 If a Boxer incurs an injury, the Referee commands the non-injured Boxer to go to the neutral corner, then asks the Ringside Doctor if the injured Boxer is fit to continue.
8.4.8 If the Ringside Doctor confirms the Boxer is fit to continue, the Referee may decide to continue the Bout.
8.4.9 If the Ringside Doctor confirms the Boxer is not fit to continue, the Referee may decide to terminate the Bout.
8.4.10 If the Referee did not see the cause of injury, the Referee requests the opinion of each of the five (5) Judges:
- All or majority saw a correct blow → RSC-I (Referee Stops Contest – Injury)
- All or majority saw an intentional foul → DSQ (Disqualification)
- All or majority saw an unintentional foul → WP (Winner on Points by scorecards)

### 8.5 The Fouls
8.5.1 Hitting below the belt (low blow).
8.5.2 Hitting with the head.
8.5.3 Hitting with the shoulder.
8.5.4 Hitting with the forearm or elbow.
8.5.5 Hitting with an open glove.
8.5.6 Hitting with the inside of the glove.
8.5.7 Hitting with the wrist or side of the hand.
8.5.8 Hitting the back of the opponent, especially on the back of the neck or head.
8.5.9 Hitting the opponent with a kidney punch.
8.5.10 Hitting the opponent with a pivot blow or backhand punch.
8.5.11 Holding.
8.5.12 Holding and hitting.
8.5.13 Locking the opponent's arm or head, or pushing an arm underneath the arm of the opponent.
8.5.14 Tripping.
8.5.15 Kicking.
8.5.16 Butting.
8.5.17 Strangling.
8.5.18 Pulling.
8.5.19 Biting.
8.5.20 Spitting on the opponent or the Referee.
8.5.21 Faking/simulating.
8.5.22 Pushing.
8.5.23 Pushing the opponent's face with an arm or an elbow.
8.5.24 Pushing the opponent's head back.
8.5.25 Attacking while holding the ropes or making any unfair use of the ropes.
8.5.26 Lying on, wrestling and throwing in the clinch.
8.5.27 Attacking an opponent who is down or who is in the act of rising.
8.5.28 Ducking below the belt of the opponent.
8.5.29 Completely passive defence by means of double cover and intentionally falling, running or turning the back to avoid a blow.
8.5.30 Speaking without the referee's permission.
8.5.31 Not stepping back when ordered to break.
8.5.32 Attempting to strike the opponent immediately after the Referee has ordered "break" and before taking a step back.
8.5.33 Assaulting or behaving in an aggressive manner towards a Referee at any time.
8.5.34 Keeping the advanced hand straight in order to obstruct the opponent's vision.

### 8.6 Knockdown
8.6.1 A Boxer is considered knocked down due to a legal blow if:
- The Boxer touches the floor with any part of the body other than the Boxer's feet as the result of a blow.
- The Boxer is lying on the ropes as the result of a blow.
- The Boxer is outside or partly outside the ropes as the result of a blow.
- Following a hard punch, the Boxer has not fallen but is considered hurt and requires a standing Eight Count.

8.6.2 In the case of a Knockdown, the Referee must say "Stop" and then begin a count from one (1) to eight (8) if the Boxer is fit to continue, or from one (1) to ten (10) if the Boxer is unfit to continue.
8.6.3 The Referee counts with intervals of one (1) second and indicates each second with the hand.
8.6.4 If both Boxers are knocked down at the same time, the count is continued as long as one of them is still knocked down.
8.6.5 Before the number "one" ("1") is counted, an interval of one (1) second must have elapsed from the time the Boxer is knocked down.
8.6.6 If a Boxer is knocked down, the Bout is continued after the count of eight (8), but the Boxer falls again without having received a further punch, the Referee may continue the count from eight (8) up to ten (10).
8.6.7 When a Boxer is knocked down, the Bout must not continue until the Referee has reached a mandatory count of eight (8), even if the Boxer is ready to continue before then or the round has ended.
8.6.8 In the case of a Boxer being knocked down at the end of any round, the Referee continues to count until the Boxer is no longer knocked down irrespective of the end of the round.
8.6.9 Should the Referee count up to ten (10), such Boxer is deemed to have lost the Bout by Knockout (KO) irrespective of the end of the round. The gong does not save the Boxer.

### 8.7 Compulsory Count Limits
8.7.1 For Elite, U19, U17 age categories:
- A maximum of three (3) Eight Counts applies in one (1) round.
- A maximum of four (4) Eight Counts applies in one (1) Bout.
8.7.2 Eight Counts due to illegal blows or fouls are not considered in Compulsory Count Limits.

### 8.8 Boxer Out of the Ring
8.8.1 If a Boxer is punched out of the ring by a legal blow, the Boxer must be allowed thirty (30) seconds to come back without the help of anyone. If unable to come back, the Boxer loses by RSC.
8.8.2 If a Boxer is pushed out of the ring, the Boxer must be allowed sixty (60) seconds to come back (no Eight Count needed). If the Boxer doesn't come back or is unfit to continue, the Ringside Doctor examines them. If unfit → opponent loses by DSQ. If fit → warning to offending Boxer and Bout resumes.
8.8.3 If a Boxer falls out of the ring due to their own accord, the Boxer must be allowed thirty (30) seconds to come back. If unable, the Boxer loses by RSC.

### 8.9 Gumshield Falling Out
8.9.1 The Referee gives a mandatory warning to a Boxer who spits out the gumshield intentionally without receiving a correct punch.
8.9.2 The Referee gives a mandatory warning to a Boxer whose gumshield falls out for the third time.

### 8.10 Low Blow
8.10.1 After a low blow, if the offended Boxer does not complain and the low blow was not hard and intentional, the Referee signals the foul without interrupting the Bout.
8.10.2 After a low blow, if the offended Boxer complains about the severity: the Referee may immediately disqualify the offending Boxer (if intentional and hard), OR the Referee may start an Eight Count.
8.10.3 After a low blow and an Eight Count, the Referee may give a warning if the offended Boxer is fit to continue, OR give a maximum of one minute and 30 seconds (90 seconds) for the offended Boxer to recover.
8.10.4 If the offended Boxer is still unfit to continue after 90 seconds, the Referee declares the opponent winner by RSC-I.

### 8.11 Caution, Warning, Disqualification
8.11.1 The Referee, at own discretion, can caution, warn or disqualify the boxer who does not obey instructions, acts against the rules, boxes in an unsportsmanlike manner, or commits fouls.
8.11.2 A third warning in a Bout for a Boxer results in an automatic disqualification.
8.11.3 When giving a warning, the Referee says "Stop" and demonstrates the infringement to the Boxer first and then to the Deputy Technical Delegate.
8.11.4 If the Referee gives a warning, the Scoring System records it and each warning reduces the total score of the offending Boxer by one (1) point per Judge.
8.11.5 The Referee gives a warning to the offending Boxer if the opponent receives a headbutt or any other serious illegal blow that does not cause an injury or a cut.
8.11.6 The Referee may disqualify the offending Boxer if the opponent receives a headbutt or other serious illegal blow deemed sufficiently serious.
8.11.7 The Referee disqualifies the offending Boxer if the opponent receives a headbutt or illegal blow that causes an injury or a cut.
8.11.8 If the Referee has any reason to believe a foul has been committed unseen, the Referee may consult the ringside Judges.
8.11.9 If the Referee discovers an irregularity in the Boxer's handwraps or bandages after the Bout that gave an advantage, the Boxer is immediately disqualified.

## Rule 9: Bout Decisions

### 9.1 Win on Points (WP)
9.1.1 At the end of a Bout, each Judge determines a winner based on the Boxer's total scores. The winner is determined through the approved Scoring System by a unanimous or split decision.
9.1.2 A unanimous decision is declared in case all five (5) Judges appoint the same winner.
9.1.3 A split decision is declared when three (3) Judges appoint one Boxer as winner and the other two either appoint the other Boxer or a draw; or four (4) Judges appoint one Boxer and the other Judge appoints the other Boxer or a draw.
9.1.4 If total scores are equal at the end of the Bout, the Judge(s) with equal scores are requested to break the tie and nominate which Boxer is the winner.
9.1.5 The Boxer who is ahead on points is declared the winner. The round in which the Bout is stopped is scored, even if it is a partial round.
9.1.6 WP applies if an injury caused by an unintentional foul occurs and the contest is stopped.
9.1.7 WP applies in case both Boxers are injured at the same time and the contest is stopped.
9.1.8 WP applies if the Bout is terminated due to a situation out of the Boxers' or Referee's control (e.g. destruction of the ring, failure of the lighting supply, forces of nature), only if the situation occurs after the end of the first round.
9.1.9 WP applies in case of a Double Knockout (DKO) during a final Bout.

### 9.2 Referee Stops Contest (RSC)
9.2.1 If a Boxer is being outclassed or receiving excessive punishment or hard blows, the Bout is stopped and the opponent is declared winner by RSC.
9.2.2 If a Boxer is standing and is unfit to continue and/or fails to resume boxing after an Eight Count, the opponent wins by RSC.
9.2.3 If a Boxer fails to resume boxing immediately after the rest period between rounds, the opponent wins by RSC.
9.2.4 If a Boxer who fell out of the ring as a result of a legal blow does not re-enter within thirty (30) seconds, the opponent wins by RSC.
9.2.5 The Ringside Doctor may advise the Referee to terminate a Bout if a Boxer is receiving excessive punishment. Opponent wins by RSC.

### 9.3 Referee Stops Contest-Injury (RSC-I)
9.3.1 If a Boxer is unfit to continue because of a sustained or increased injury from legal blows, the Bout is stopped and the opponent wins by RSC-I.
9.3.2 If a Boxer is unfit to continue because of an injury not sustained from blows, the Bout is stopped and the opponent wins by RSC-I.
9.3.3 If a Boxer suffers a low blow and does not recover after one minute and 30 seconds (1m 30s), the opponent wins by RSC-I.

### 9.4 Abandonment (ABD)
9.4.1 If a Boxer retires voluntarily or if the Second throws the towel into the ring (except while the Referee is conducting a count), the opponent wins by ABD.
9.4.2 If a Boxer loses a contact lens during a round or Bout and does not accept to continue without the contact lens, the Referee terminates the Bout and declares the opponent winner by ABD.

### 9.5 Win by Disqualification (DSQ)
9.5.1 If a Boxer is disqualified for any reason, the opponent wins by DSQ.
9.5.2 If the Referee determines that an intentional foul caused an injury and the fouled Boxer cannot continue because of the injury, the offending Boxer is disqualified.
9.5.3 The third warning in the Bout automatically disqualifies the Boxer; opponent wins by DSQ.
9.5.4 If both Boxers are disqualified, both lose by BDSQ.
9.5.5 If both Boxers are disqualified before a semi-final, their opponent in the next round wins by walkover.
9.5.6 If both Boxers are disqualified during a final Bout (BDSQ), both Boxers will be awarded silver medals.

### 9.6 Disqualification for Unsportsmanlike Behaviour (DQB)
9.6.1 If a Boxer is disqualified for Unsportsmanlike Behaviour (such as assaulting or acting aggressively towards a Referee, Judge, Technical Delegate, Team Official, opponent or other), the opponent wins by DQB and the disqualified Boxer is subject to sanctions by World Boxing.
9.6.2 The Referee declares a winner by DSQ and only the Technical Delegate has the authority to adjust the result to DQB.
9.6.3 If both Boxers are disqualified for Unsportsmanlike Behaviour, neither Boxer will be eligible for a medal.

### 9.7 Win by Knockout (KO)
9.7.1 If a Boxer is knocked down and fails to stand and resume boxing before being counted to ten (10), the opponent wins by KO.
9.7.2 In the case of an emergency and the Referee suspending the count and/or summoning the Ringside Doctor before the Boxer is counted to ten (10), the opponent wins by KO.
9.7.3 After the Referee has said "Ten" ("10"), the Bout ends and must be decided as KO. The Referee may stop the count at any point if the Boxer needs immediate medical attention, and the decision will be KO.
9.7.4 In the case a Double KO (DKO) occurs, both Boxers lose by DKO.
9.7.5 If a DKO occurs during a final, the WP decision applies.

### 9.8 Win by Walkover (WO)
9.8.1 If a Boxer is present in the ring fully attired and ready to box, and the opposing Boxer fails to appear after a maximum period of one (1) minute has elapsed after the bell has been sounded, the Referee declares the present Boxer winner by WO.
9.8.2 If a Boxer fails the Medical Examination or Daily Weigh-In, the opponent wins by WO.
9.8.3 No medal will be awarded to a Boxer who has not boxed at least once within the entire Competition period.
9.8.4 If a Boxer who won by DSQ is declared unfit to compete in the next round, then the opponent wins by WO.

### 9.10 Bout Protest
9.10.1 No protest or appeal in World Boxing Competitions is permitted and bout decisions are final.

## Rule 10: The Technical Officials (TOs)

### 10.6 The Ringside Doctor
10.6.1 Ringside Doctors must be certified by World Boxing to be appointed to any World Boxing Competition.
10.6.2 A minimum of one (1) and a maximum of two (2) Ringside Doctors will be present ringside throughout all Sessions.
10.6.6 The Ringside Doctor will be present one (1) hour before the first Bout and until they check the Boxers at the end of the last Bout of each competition day.
10.6.7 The Ringside Doctor will wear surgical gloves during the Bout.
10.6.9 The Ringside Doctor may treat an injury for a maximum of one (1) minute. Treatment may only take place once per Bout for each Boxer.
10.6.13 If a Boxer is rendered unconscious, only the Referee and the Ringside Doctor may remain in the ring.
10.6.14 In case a Boxer is unconscious for more than one (1) minute, the Boxer must be transported to the nearest hospital for further evaluation. Any Boxer with a concussion may be referred to the hospital.
10.6.15 In case of a KO without unconsciousness, or in case of an RSC, the Boxer will be examined by the Ringside Doctor immediately afterwards in the locker room.
10.6.17 The Ringside Doctor decides if a Boxer is "fit to box".
10.6.18 The Ringside Doctor fills out the Medical Bout Report in case a restriction period has been ordered.
10.6.19 Ringside Doctors must make a request to the DTD or TD to stop the Bout if a competing Boxer appears unable to continue.

## Rule 12: Disciplinary Measures

### 12.2 Sanctions for Prohibited Actions by R&Js and TOs
12.2.1 Any R&J or TO may be sanctioned at any time based on their behaviour.
12.2.2 A decision on suspension(s) related to R&Js' or TOs' ethics must be made by the World Boxing Ethics Chief.

### 12.3 Sanctions for Prohibited Actions by Seconds
12.3.2 For misconduct on the Field of Play:
12.3.2.1 First violation → caution
12.3.2.2 Second violation → warning; placed just outside the Field of Play area
12.3.2.3 Third violation → removed by the Technical Delegate for the rest of the day
12.3.2.4 If removed a second time → completely suspended from the Competition

## Rule 13: Boxing Equipment

### 13.1 Gloves
13.1.1 Boxers must wear red or blue gloves according to their corner allocation.
13.1.3 The gloves must be taken off by Boxers immediately after the Bout is over and before the decision is announced.
13.1.4 The padding of the gloves must not be displaced or broken. Only clean and serviceable gloves must be used.
13.1.5 Gloves must weigh 284 grams (approx. 10oz) or 340 grams (approx. 12oz), with a tolerance of 5%. The leather portion must not weigh more than half the total weight, and the padding portion not less than half.
13.1.6 Ten (10) oz gloves will be used for:
- All Women's Age and Weight Categories
- All U17 Men's Weight Categories
- Elite Men's M51kg (Flyweight) to M67kg (Welterweight)
- U19 Men's M50kg (Flyweight) to M65kg (Welterweight)
13.1.7 Twelve (12) oz gloves will be used for:
- Elite Men's M71kg (Light Middleweight) to M92+kg (Super Heavyweight)
- U19 Men's M70kg (Light Middleweight) to M90+kg (Super Heavyweight)
13.1.8 Only gloves with Velcro closing systems will be used for all World Boxing Competitions.
13.1.9 The glove closure system will be covered by 5cm (2in) wide glove tape, maximum one layer around the wrist.

### 13.2 Professional Handwraps and Velcro Bandages
13.2.1 In U17 and U19 Boxing Competitions, Boxers must use Velcro bandages.
13.2.2 In Elite Boxing Competitions, Boxers must use either Velcro bandages or professional handwraps, as specified in Event Rules.
13.2.3 In any World Boxing Competition, all Boxers must use the same type of hand protection.
13.2.5 Velcro bandages: Two 3.5-4.5m long and 5.7 cm wide stretchy cotton material rolls with a Velcro closure.
13.2.6 Professional handwraps specifications: 2 rolls of 5cm x 15m gauze; 2 rolls of 5cm x 10m gauze; 2 rolls of 2.5cm x 13m zinc oxide tape; 1 roll of 1.25cm x 13m zinc oxide tape for between the fingers.
13.2.7 It is prohibited to use any substance and foreign materials on the professional handwraps and Velcro bandages.
13.2.8 For professional handwraps, it is also prohibited to: cover the knuckles with zinc oxide tape; stack gauze and zinc oxide tape; place zinc oxide tape directly on the skin.

### 13.3 Headguards
13.3.1 In all U17, U19 Boxing Competitions, Boxers must wear a red or blue headguard as per their corner.
13.3.2 In Elite Women's Boxing Competitions, Boxers must wear a red or blue headguard as per their corner.
13.3.3 In Elite Men's Boxing Competitions, a Boxer must NOT wear a headguard.
13.3.4 Seconds must ensure that the Boxer's hair is secured appropriately. Hair extending outside of the headguard must not exceed 5cm.
13.3.7 The headguard must weigh a maximum of 450 grams (approx. 16oz).
13.3.9 Only headguards with Velcro closing systems will be approved for all World Boxing Competitions.
13.3.10 The padding of the headguards must respect a minimum thickness of 2-3cm.

### 13.4 Kinesiology Tape
13.4.1 Boxers may use kinesiology taping, as long as it is only applied:
- From the waist down, and
- To the back of the body.

### 13.5 Competition Uniform
13.5.1 Boxers must wear uniforms predominantly of the colour corresponding to their respective corner (red or blue).
13.5.2 Boxers must wear a vest and shorts or a skirt. The length of the shorts or skirt must not be shorter than mid-thigh, must not cover the knee, and must not be worn above the belt line.
13.5.3 The belt line is an imaginary line from the navel to the top of the hips, and must not cover the navel. The belt line must be clearly identified by a contrasting colour and must be 5-10cm wide.
13.5.12 All forms of advertising for sponsors of political, religious or personal statements, or alcohol (except beer and wine), tobacco, are prohibited on all kit items inside the Competition Venue.
13.5.14 No tape or binding device may be used for securing the competition uniform.

### 13.6 Shoes and Socks
13.6.1 A Boxer must wear athletic footwear such as shoes or light boots without spikes or heels.
13.6.2 If a Boxer wears socks, the socks cannot cover the knee.

### 13.7 Gumshield
13.7.1 All Boxers must wear a Gumshield during the Bout, and any colour of mouthpiece may be worn. The gumshield should be form-fitted.
13.7.2 If a Boxer is wearing dental braces, they can box but must have a form-fitting Gumshield.

### 13.8 Groin and Breast Protectors
13.8.1 Men Boxers must wear a groin protector during a Bout.
13.8.2 Women Boxers may use a groin protector during a Bout.
13.8.3 If a woman Boxer wears a breast protector, it must not have any metal parts except for the fastening site at the back. The breast protector must follow the natural contours of the breasts and must not cover any target area of the body, including the sternum.

### 13.9 Hijab
13.9.1 Hijab can be used in Boxing Competitions by women Boxers.
13.9.4 The hijab must be coloured black and include: Long-sleeved form-fitting shirt under the Competition vest; Full-length form-fitting tights under the Competition shorts or skirt; Sport hijab scarf.

### 13.10 Contact Lenses
13.10.1 A Boxer may wear soft contact lenses.
13.10.2 Hard contact lenses are prohibited.

### 13.11 Body Accessories
13.11.1 No body piercing or jewellery is permitted to be worn during a Bout.
13.11.2 No additional body accessories (such as elbow pads) are allowed on the Boxer's upper body during a Bout, unless otherwise authorised.

### 13.12 Boxing Ring
13.12.1 The ring must measure a minimum 6.10m inside the ropes on all four (4) sides, and the apron of the ring must extend a minimum of 61cm on each side. The overall length and width of the ring platform must be a minimum of 7.4m.
13.12.2 The platform of the ring must be at a height of 100cm from the ground.
13.12.3 The ring surface must have padding comprised of rubber or other suitable material. The thickness of the padding must be between 1.5cm to 3.0cm.
13.12.6 The ring must include four (4) corner posts, four (4) ropes on all sides, and four (4) corner pads.
13.12.7 The ropes must be made of material minimum 4cm in diameter covered in thick padding. Heights from canvas: 40cm, 70cm, 100cm and 130cm.
13.12.9 Corner pad arrangement facing the Technical Delegate: near left side – red; far left side – white; far right side – blue; near right side – white.

## Rule 14: Slogans, Statements, Images and Advertising

14.1.1 Equipment must not have any political, religious, or personal slogans, inappropriate statements or images.
14.1.2 Boxers and other Team Members must not have clothing or reveal undergarments that show political, religious, personal slogans, statements or images, or advertising other than the manufacturer's logo.
14.1.5 No kind of demonstration or political, religious or racial propaganda is permitted at any Event sites, venues or other areas.
